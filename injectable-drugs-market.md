<p><p>The injectable drugs market consists of various delivery formats including ampoules, vials, cartridges, and bottles. Ampoules are small sealed containers that protect sensitive drugs from contamination. Vials are glass or plastic containers often used for multi-dose or single-dose medications. Cartridges are pre-filled units commonly used in pen injectors for diabetes management. Bottles are larger containers that can hold more substantial volumes of liquid drugs, often utilized for vaccines and intravenous therapies. Each type serves unique needs in pharmaceutical delivery.</p></p>
