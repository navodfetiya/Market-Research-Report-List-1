<p><p>The hip orthoses market includes dynamic and static orthotics, each serving distinct functions. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide movement assistance and support during rehabilitation, facilitating mobility and functional activity. They often incorporate adjustable components to adapt to a patient's progress. In contrast, static orthotics offer rigid support, immobilizing the hip joint to promote healing in cases of fractures or post-surgery recovery. Both types are crucial in managing hip conditions, enhancing recovery outcomes and improving quality of life.</p></p>
