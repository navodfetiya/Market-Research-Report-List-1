<p><p>Bio-sensors for non-clinical applications are devices that detect biological substances and convert them into measurable signals. The market can be segmented into piezoelectric, thermal, optical, and electrochemical types. Piezoelectric bio-sensors utilize mechanical stress to generate electrical signals, while thermal bio-sensors measure temperature changes caused by biochemical reactions. Optical bio-sensors rely on light absorption or fluorescence for detection, and electrochemical bio-sensors analyze changes in electrical signals due to chemical interactions. Each type offers unique advantages for various industrial and environmental applications.</p></p>
