<p><p>Active Harmonic Filters (AHFs) are advanced devices designed to mitigate harmonic distortion caused by non-linear loads in electrical systems. By actively monitoring the electrical network and injecting counteracting currents, these filters enhance power quality, protect equipment, and improve energy efficiency. AHFs are increasingly deployed in various sectors, including manufacturing, telecommunications, and renewable energy, owing to their efficacy in providing real-time harmonic compensation.</p><p>The Active Harmonic Filter Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.7% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the rising awareness of power quality issues, stringent regulations for energy efficiency, and the increasing integration of renewable energy sources that generate harmonics. Additionally, advancements in semiconductor technology and the development of compact and cost-effective filter solutions are further propelling market expansion. </p><p>Emerging trends include the adoption of smart grid technologies, which necessitate robust power quality management solutions, and increased investment in industrial automation. The demand for AHFs is also being boosted by the growing need for compliance with international standards, as businesses strive to enhance the reliability and performance of their electrical systems. Overall, the Active Harmonic Filter Market is poised for significant growth in the coming years.</p></p>
