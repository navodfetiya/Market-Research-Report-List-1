<p><p>The cooking oils and fats market is primarily divided into two types: vegetable and animal. Vegetable oils are derived from plants, such as seeds and nuts, and are often favored for their health benefits and high smoking points, making them suitable for frying and baking. Common examples include olive, sunflower, and canola oils. Animal fats are sourced from livestock, like lard from pigs or tallow from cattle, and are traditionally used for their rich flavor and high-temperature cooking properties. Each type serves varied culinary uses and preferences.</p></p>
